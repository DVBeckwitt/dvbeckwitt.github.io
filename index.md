## Current focus

My dissertation, _Investigating Disorder in van der Waals Thin Films_, asks how much structural information can be learned from two-dimensional diffraction images before reducing them to one-dimensional curves.

I work with ordered Bi<sub>2</sub>Te<sub>3</sub> and Bi<sub>2</sub>Se<sub>3</sub> films and CVD-grown PbI<sub>2</sub> films to separate geometry, alignment, mosaicity, phase content, and stacking disorder. The central object is the detector image: specular spots, off-specular arcs, and diffuse intensity each preserve information that a simple line cut can lose.

## Research software

I maintain tools that grew from real detector and modeling problems: [2D_Mosaic_Sim](https://github.com/DVBeckwitt/2D_Mosaic_Sim) for orientation-dependent diffraction simulation, [ra_sim](https://github.com/DVBeckwitt/ra_sim) for R-Axis IV++ simulation and analysis, and [OSC_Reader](https://github.com/DVBeckwitt/OSC_Reader) for converting proprietary detector files into inspectable formats.

The common theme is practical structure analysis. The software is meant to make scattering data easier to simulate, inspect, and reason about before compressing it into a smaller set of fitted parameters.
