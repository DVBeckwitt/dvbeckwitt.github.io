## Current role

I work in Dr. Paul Miceli's group as a Graduate Research Assistant. I operate custom X-ray diffraction and reflectivity systems, collect area-detector data, and build models that connect image morphology to structure.

My dissertation focuses on van der Waals films with partial order: aligned layer normals, disordered in-plane orientation, mosaic spread, and stacking faults.

## Background

I earned an M.S. in Physics from the University of Missouri in 2022 and a B.S. in Physics from Missouri State University in 2020, with minors in Mathematics and Chemistry.

Before my current graduate research, I worked on graphene, oxide thin films, pulsed laser deposition, vapor deposition, Raman spectroscopy, SEM, EDS, profilometry, and diffraction. I also developed biomedical testing software and hardware automation as an R&D intern at Dynatek Labs.

## Teaching and public work

I have tutored and taught physics courses ranging from small advanced sections to large introductory lectures. My teaching emphasizes clear expectations, active practice, collaborative problem solving, demonstrations, simulations, and useful feedback.

My outreach work includes MU Extension eclipse events, STEM Cubs, Moberly Correctional Center, Columbia Young Scientist Expo, high school physics demonstrations, and student mentoring.
