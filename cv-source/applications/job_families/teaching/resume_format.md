# Teaching Resume Format

Use this for physics lecturer, instructor, visiting lecturer, assistant teaching professor, teaching assistant professor, professor of practice, adjunct physics faculty, lab instructor, academic tutor, STEM education specialist, learning-support specialist, outreach or education-program coordinator, and teaching-adjacent roles.

This file is not lecturer-only. It provides a general teaching resume framework plus a specific one-page lecturer branch for cases like one-year undergraduate physics appointments.

## Strategy selector

Choose the resume type from the role and David's request.

### One-page teaching-focused lecturer resume

Use when the role is a lecturer, instructor, visiting lecturer, one-year lecturer, undergraduate faculty role, or similar teaching-first appointment and David asks for a resume, one-page resume, or targeted resume.

Core rule: optimize for immediate teaching fit, student support, lab and course relevance, and undergraduate-facing research fit. Do not optimize for maximum research detail or prestige.

If the posting asks for a CV but David asks for a resume, produce the resume and note separately that a full CV may still be required.

### General teaching resume

Use for academic tutor, lab instructor, STEM education specialist, learning-support specialist, outreach coordinator, education-program coordinator, and other student-facing teaching roles where a compact resume is more useful than an academic CV.

Lead with the evidence most relevant to the role: tutoring and student support for learning-support roles, lab and course support for lab-instructor roles, public communication and program work for outreach roles, or active-learning classroom practice for teaching roles.

### Two-page teaching resume

Use when the role is teaching-heavy but the posting values a wider evidence base, such as course coverage, teaching methods, tutoring, service, outreach, workshops, awards, and selected research fit.

### Teaching-focused CV

Use when David did not request a one-page resume and the posting specifically asks for a CV, publications, presentations, teaching philosophy, evidence of teaching effectiveness, academic review, or a full faculty package.

Keep teaching before research unless the role is explicitly research-teaching balanced.

## Universal teaching rules

- Put the strongest student-facing evidence in the top third.
- Use course names, student levels, enrollment ranges, teaching modes, and methods when supported.
- Keep claims evidence-based and ATS-friendly.
- Use research only when it helps the teaching argument: undergraduate projects, lab activities, computational examples, curriculum connections, course-connected scholarship, or disciplinary credibility.
- Do not make a teaching resume read like a compressed research CV unless the posting is research-teaching balanced.

## Claim fidelity

Direct claims may be stated plainly when supported:

- `taught and assisted`
- `used active-learning practices`
- `provided tutoring`
- `recognized with the Green Chalk Teaching Award`

Adjacent claims must be framed as fit, preparation, potential contribution, or transferable experience:

- `prepared to support`
- `can contribute to`
- `project areas can include`
- `supports undergraduate-accessible projects`
- `can connect to laboratory or computational physics activities`

Omit weak or unevidenced assertions rather than adding keywords without evidence.

## Claims to avoid unless verified

Do not claim completed Ph.D., ABD status, instructor of record, independent course ownership, formal academic advising, faculty committee service, postdoctoral experience, PI status, external funding awards, student evaluation scores, pass rates, retention effects, measured learning gains, modern physics teaching ownership, optics teaching ownership, teaching license, AP Physics, IB, dual-credit teaching, formal curriculum mapping, LMS ownership, online-course ownership, or reference contact details without explicit verification.

## Summary

Use 2 to 3 compact lines. Name the teaching context, strongest student-facing evidence, and one role-relevant differentiator.

Lecturer example:

Physics Ph.D. candidate, University of Missouri, expected May 2026, with undergraduate physics teaching and tutoring experience. Taught and assisted introductory algebra- and calculus-based mechanics, electricity and magnetism, and classical mechanics using active-learning practices and student support. X-ray and detector-data research can support undergraduate-accessible computational and materials-physics projects.

Tutoring or learning-support example:

Physics Ph.D. candidate and undergraduate physics tutor with experience supporting students across mechanics, electricity and magnetism, and problem-solving courses. Uses background-aware explanations, office-hour-style support, and active-learning habits to make physics concepts more accessible.

Outreach or education-program example:

Physics educator and scientific communicator with undergraduate teaching, tutoring, workshop, and public STEM outreach experience. Translates physics, X-ray scattering, and materials-analysis concepts for students and mixed-experience audiences.

Avoid dissertation-first framing, dense technical research language, independent faculty course ownership claims, and unsupported advising or retention claims.

## Research and Teaching Fit

Choose the section title based on the role:

- `Research and Undergraduate Project Fit` for teaching-focused lecturer roles.
- `Research and Laboratory Fit` for lab-instructor or curriculum/lab roles.
- `Scientific Communication and Outreach Fit` for outreach or education-program roles.
- `Research and Technical Expertise` for research-teaching balanced roles.

Use research to support the teaching strategy, not to overwhelm it.

Reusable bullets:

- X-ray diffraction and reflectivity research can support undergraduate-accessible projects in thin-film materials, diffraction, measurement uncertainty, detector-image inspection, and scientific plotting.
- Python modeling and detector-data analysis can contribute to computational physics activities using notebooks, reproducible plots, XRD/XRR fitting examples, and error-propagation exercises.
- Machine-learning-assisted structure analysis supports bounded project areas where students can connect simulation, data interpretation, and materials physics.
- Workshops, invited talks, and outreach translate advanced X-ray and machine-learning concepts for students, researchers, and mixed-experience audiences.

Compress or remove long lists of fitted parameters, manuscript-level detail, facility-development plans, grant language, and equipment build-out plans for teaching-first resumes.

Do not imply past undergraduate supervision, guaranteed access to external facilities, reduced teaching load for research, independent curriculum ownership, wholesale curriculum replacement, or current institutional instrumentation unless verified.

## Service and Outreach

Use service selectively based on role fit.

- Directed PhysAssist and served in Physics and Astronomy Graduate Student Association and Graduate Professional Council roles supporting student and department community.
- Contributed to STEM outreach and public engagement through physics demonstrations, MU Extension eclipse events, STEM Cubs, Moberly Correctional Center, Columbia Young Scientist Expo, and workshops when relevant.
- Served as CGW Diversity Officer and participated in graduate-student leadership when role-relevant.

Avoid faculty committee-service language unless verified.

## Selected Skills

Tailor to the posting and keep unsupported keywords out of standalone skills lists.

Teaching: undergraduate physics instruction, tutoring, active learning, think-pair-share, peer instruction, inquiry tutorials, demonstrations, simulations, office hours, discussion-board support

Course coverage: algebra- and calculus-based mechanics, electricity and magnetism, classical mechanics, physics problem solving, laboratory-oriented examples

Student support and communication: background-aware explanations, small-group support, workshops, outreach, public science communication, interdisciplinary examples

Physics and computation: XRD/XRR, thin-film materials, Python, Jupyter, NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib, reproducible data analysis

Only add modern physics, optics, formal advising, curriculum ownership, or laboratory-course ownership if direct evidence supports those claims for the application.

## One-page compression rules

For any requested one-page teaching resume:

- Header: two lines max.
- Summary: three compact lines.
- Education: two to three lines when possible.
- Teaching or student-support evidence: four to five bullets total.
- Research or technical fit: three bullets max.
- Service or outreach: two compressed bullets.
- Skills: two lines.

Cut in this order if over page:

1. Target Role line.
2. Research technical subdetails.
3. Duplicate active-learning terms.
4. Long outreach lists.
5. Publications.
6. Extra skills.

Never cut Ph.D. candidate expected completion date, directly relevant teaching or student-support evidence, active learning, tutoring, the Green Chalk Teaching Award when teaching is central, or undergraduate-facing research fit before less central content.
