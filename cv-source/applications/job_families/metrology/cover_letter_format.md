# Metrology Cover Letter Format

Use one page. Keep the letter focused on measurement, instrumentation, analysis, and reproducibility.

## Paragraph 1

State the role and company. Position David as a Physics Ph.D. candidate with quantitative X-ray diffraction, reflectivity instrumentation, thin-film characterization, and Python analysis experience. Add one company-specific sentence from the posting or product area.

Template:

I am applying for the [role] position at [company]. I am a Ph.D. candidate in Physics at the University of Missouri, expected May 2026, with experience operating custom X-ray diffraction and reflectivity systems, building Python-based analysis workflows, and using quantitative scattering methods to characterize thin-film materials. [Company-specific sentence tied to the role's measurement domain.]

## Paragraph 2

Primary proof point: instrument operation and quantitative analysis.

Use details such as custom-built XRD/XRR systems, GIWAXS modeling, detector geometry, site occupancy, Debye-Waller factors, mosaicity, stacking faults, and defect density.

## Paragraph 3

Secondary proof point: software, automation, and materials context.

Use 2D_Mosaic_Sim, ra_sim, OSC_Reader, Python, NumPy/SciPy/pandas, PyTorch, CVD, PLD, Raman, SEM/EDS, and profilometry only as relevant.

## Paragraph 4

Close by connecting measurement discipline, instrument-user perspective, and technical communication to the employer's need.

## Cautions

If the posting requires regulated calibration, quality systems, production metrology, or semiconductor fab methods, acknowledge adjacent measurement-science experience rather than claiming direct experience.
