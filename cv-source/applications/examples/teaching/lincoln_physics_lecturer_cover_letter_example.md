Re: One Year Lecturer of Physics
Dear Members of the Search Committee:
I am applying for the One Year Lecturer of Physics position at Lincoln University of Missouri. I am a PhD candidate in
condensed matter physics at the University of Missouri with six years of experience in undergraduate physics
instruction, experimental design, computational modeling, and materials characterization. Lincoln's mission as
Missouri's only public HBCU, and the department's emphasis on inclusive excellence, student success, and expanding
participation in STEM among underrepresented populations, match the priorities that guide my teaching and
mentoring.
My teaching experience includes instructor and teaching assistant roles in algebra-based and calculus-based
mechanics, which align directly with the introductory physics courses listed in the posting. I can also support upper-
level undergraduate courses in mechanics, electricity and magnetism, modern physics, optics, and computational
physics. In the classroom, I use structured problem solving, low-stakes formative assessment, think-pair-share,
anonymous polling, flipped preparation, inquiry-based activities, and accessible office hours. These evidence-based
teaching practices help students connect equations, experiments, and applications in pre-engineering, chemistry,
mathematics, and related STEM pathways. My teaching has been recognized with the Excellence in Undergraduate
Teaching Award and the student-driven Green Chalk Teaching Award.
I would contribute to curriculum development, laboratory modernization, academic advising, mentoring, and
committee service from the start of the appointment. My teaching philosophy is student-centered and high-
expectation, with deliberate scaffolding, frequent feedback, and a course culture in which office hours, peer
discussion, and revision are normal parts of learning physics. I have also organized demonstration days for local
schools, volunteered at observatories, and directed a graduate-student mental-health program, experiences that
would translate well to departmental initiatives and student-success programming at Lincoln.
My research program can be adapted into undergraduate-accessible projects. I study structural characterization of
van der Waals thin films using grazing-incidence X-ray and neutron scattering. I have developed Monte Carlo
simulations to extract occupancies, defect densities, and anisotropic Debye-Waller factors, built convolutional neural
networks for automated structure analysis, and grown phase-controlled two-dimensional films by chemical vapor
deposition. At Lincoln, I would involve undergraduates in scattering-data analysis, simulations of lattice disorder,
image-based structure classification, and materials characterization. Where appropriate, I would seek external
funding to support undergraduate research, equipment, and student conference participation.
I would welcome the opportunity to contribute to physics teaching, undergraduate research, academic advising, and
student success at Lincoln University of Missouri. Thank you for considering my application.
Sincerely,

David V. Beckwitt
