This is a revised style reference based on David's Beamport Engineer direction. Use it to calibrate voice, not as an automatic evidence source or as text to copy into unrelated applications.

Key voice traits:

- Direct first-person opening.
- Specific technical identity before broad motivation.
- Concrete mapping between the role and evidence already supported.
- Honest boundary-setting when the role uses adjacent experience.
- Practical close without sales language.

## Representative excerpt

I am a Physics Ph.D. candidate at the University of Missouri, expected July 2026, specializing in quantitative X-ray diffraction, reflectivity instrumentation, GIWAXS modeling, and area-detector scattering analysis. I am interested in the Beamport Engineer role with the University of Missouri Research Reactor because it depends on the same habits I use in my current work: keeping scattering instruments useful, turning detector data into reliable structural information, and communicating technical limits clearly.

I operate and maintain our in-house X-ray diffraction and reflectivity systems, and I build Python-based GIWAXS models that extract site occupancies, Debye-Waller factors, mosaicity, geometric parameters, stacking-fault information, and defect-density information from area-detector data. I have not held a formal neutron beamline operations role, but I do bring the core habits the Beamport Engineer role asks for: instrument-adjacent troubleshooting, quantitative detector-data analysis, scientific programming, and careful communication with technical users.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my scattering instrumentation, scientific programming, and detector-data analysis background can support MURR's neutron scattering team.
