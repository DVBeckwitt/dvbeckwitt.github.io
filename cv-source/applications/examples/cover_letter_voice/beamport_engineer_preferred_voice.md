# Beamport Engineer Preferred Voice Example

This is a style reference from David's Beamport Engineer revision. Use it to calibrate voice, not as an automatic evidence source or as text to copy into unrelated applications.

Key voice traits:

- Direct first-person opening.
- Specific technical identity before broad motivation.
- Concrete overlap between the role and work already performed.
- Honest boundary-setting when the role uses adjacent experience.
- Practical close without sales language.

## Representative excerpt

I am applying for the Beamport Engineer position with the University of Missouri Research Reactor. I am a Physics Ph.D. candidate at the University of Missouri, expected July 2026, specializing in quantitative X-ray diffraction, reflectivity instrumentation, GIWAXS modeling, and area-detector scattering analysis. As such, MURR's role is close to the work and duties I already perform under direction of my advisor Dr. Paul Miceli, who serves on the MURR advisory committee.

My graduate research has centered on scattering measurements. I operate and maintain our in-house X-ray diffraction and reflectivity systems, and I build Python-based GIWAXS models that extract site occupancies, Debye-Waller factors, mosaicity, geometric parameters, stacking-fault and defect-density information from area-detector data. That experience is directly relevant to maintaining and advancing MURR's neutron scattering instruments and other beam-port equipment, even though my operational background is X-ray rather than direct neutron beamline operation.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my scattering instrumentation, scientific programming, and detector-data analysis background can support MURR's neutron scattering team.
