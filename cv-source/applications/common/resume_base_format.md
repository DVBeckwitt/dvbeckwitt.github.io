# Resume Base Format

Use plain ATS-friendly formatting. Avoid columns, icons, graphics, photos, skill bars, unusual fonts, dense academic prose, and decorative layouts.

Always apply `application_style_calibration.md` before finalizing a resume. The default resume should be practical, concrete, and concise. It should read like a technical application document, not like an academic CV rewrite.

Do not include private phone numbers or addresses unless David provides them for the specific application.

Do not add a `Target role:` line to the resume header unless David asks for it. Use the exact role title in the file name and cover letter `Re:` line instead.

## Section-heading style

For one-page technical, instrumentation, facility, metrology-adjacent, analyst, and nonacademic resumes, use uppercase plain headings by default:

- `SUMMARY`
- `CORE SKILLS`
- `TECHNICAL EXPERIENCE`
- `APPLIED RESEARCH TOOLS`, `PROJECTS`, or another role-specific tools section
- `TEACHING AND TECHNICAL COMMUNICATION`, when communication, tutoring, workshops, or teaching help the role
- `EDUCATION`

For academic teaching CVs or formal academic dossiers, use the teaching job-family instructions instead of forcing this one-page industry style.

## Summary

Use 2 to 4 compact lines. Name David's current identity, strongest role-relevant evidence, and practical strengths. Do not use generic adjectives.

For resumes, do not default to first person. Use first person only when David requests it or when a transition summary would otherwise sound artificial. Cover letters should use first person.

Default practical technical frame:

`Physics Ph.D. candidate at the University of Missouri, expected July 2026, with hands-on experience [direct technical evidence], [secondary evidence], and [software or workflow evidence].`

`Strengths are experience with [practical habit], [validation habit], [documentation habit], and troubleshooting by comparing observed system behavior with expected physical or technical behavior.`

For AI or ML claims, lead with the scientific workflow, simulation, validation, and research outcome rather than the tool name.

For teaching roles, choose the correct branch before drafting: teaching-focused lecturer resume, general teaching resume, tutoring or learning-support resume, outreach or education-program resume, two-page teaching resume, or teaching-focused CV.

## CV-aligned summary rule

The summary must select one primary CV evidence cluster and one secondary cluster. Do not try to summarize the whole CV.

Use one of these default frames and adjust to the posting:

Scientific or metrology:

Physics Ph.D. candidate at the University of Missouri, expected July 2026, with hands-on experience operating and maintaining custom X-ray diffraction and reflectivity systems, building Python-based detector-data workflows, and validating measurements against physical models.

Data, ML, or analyst:

Quantitative physicist with 7+ years of Python experience building simulation, optimization, visualization, and scientific ML workflows for complex detector data.

AI-assisted scientific computing:

Physics Ph.D. candidate using AI-assisted scientific-computing workflows for code scaffolding, documentation, literature triage, reproducibility checks, and validation planning, with interpretation checked against physical simulation, detector geometry, and scattering constraints.

Teaching:

Physics Ph.D. candidate with undergraduate physics teaching and tutoring experience, active-learning methods, and student-support practices across mechanics, electricity and magnetism, and classical mechanics.

Applications or scientific sales:

Physics Ph.D. candidate with hands-on X-ray scattering instrumentation experience, Python analysis, teaching, workshops, invited talks, and public-facing technical communication.

Avoid summaries that imply direct industry ownership not shown in the CV, such as production ML engineer, commercial AI product owner, QA engineer, metrology engineer with regulated calibration ownership, sales professional, People Analytics specialist, or instructor of record.

## Core skills

Group skills by employer need. Put the most relevant group first.

For one-page practical technical resumes, prefer label-led lines over a bulleted skill list:

`Instrumentation and lab systems: custom XRD/XRR operation and maintenance, thin-film growth systems, PLD/PVD, CVD, Raman spectroscopy, SEM/EDS, profilometry, electron microscopy`

`Measurement and diagnostics: detector geometry, measurement validation, expected-output comparisons, reproducible data collection, scientific image inspection, model-data comparison`

`Software and data: automated hardware-system software, Python 7+ years, NumPy, pandas, SciPy, Matplotlib, Jupyter, Git, SQL, C++, Fortran, Excel/VBA, MATLAB, OriginLab`

`Communication: technical writing, presentations, workshops, teaching, tutoring, documentation and reproducibility checks`

Change these labels for the posting only when a different grouping makes the role fit clearer.

## Experience

Put the most relevant role first. Translate research into employer needs, but keep each claim evidence-based.

For transition roles, translate carefully: Python research tools can become automation, validation scripts, and reproducible workflows; X-ray and instrument troubleshooting can become technical debugging across software, data, and hardware-adjacent systems; teaching and tutoring can become technical explanation and user support. Do not imply direct product, enterprise, QA, reactor, sales, data, or engineering ownership unless supported.

Bullet formula:

Verb + method/tool + technical object + purpose.

For practical technical roles, prefer concise bullets that emphasize operation, maintenance, diagnostics, validation, documentation, and reproducibility over long research-detail lists.

Strong practical bullet patterns:

- `Operate and maintain [system] for [purpose], supporting stable data collection and reproducible measurements.`
- `Build Python-based [workflow] that convert, inspect, simulate, and analyze [data type].`
- `Diagnose unexpected [system or measurement] behavior using [evidence], [model], [context], and model-data comparisons before drawing conclusions.`
- `Use documentation, code repositories, and validation planning to keep [workflow] reproducible and reviewable.`

For AI-assisted work, add the validation boundary: physics-based model, detector geometry, scattering constraint, reproducibility check, or documented review step.

## CV-supported bullet rules

Prefer verbs that match the CV:

- operate and maintain
- build
- extract
- model
- quantify
- grew, for completed film-growth work
- develop
- convert
- visualize
- diagnose
- validate
- document
- teach and assist
- provide tutoring
- present
- support
- direct or serve in, for service roles only

Use `own`, `manage`, `lead`, `deploy`, `deliver`, `improve`, `reduce`, `increase`, `standardize`, or metric-based claims only when the evidence matrix contains direct support.

Do not add metrics such as sample throughput, number of users supported, same-day turnaround, defect reduction, model accuracy, business impact, revenue, retention, student outcomes, or production deployment unless David provides them for that application.

Every bullet should map to one of these forms:

Direct technical:

Verb + supported method/tool + supported object + supported purpose.

Adjacent translation:

Supported technical habit + target-role relevance + clear boundary.

Teaching:

Taught/assisted/provided tutoring + supported course or setting + supported method or student-support practice.

Communication:

Presented/taught/workshopped/explained + supported topic + supported audience or setting.

## Projects or applied research tools

Use when software, modeling, data analysis, or instrumentation work is central to the role.

For practical technical resumes, keep project descriptions short and use lowercase verbs after the project name:

- `Detector Data Pipeline: reads proprietary OSC detector images and converts them into accessible formats for inspection and downstream analysis.`
- `2D Powder Simulator and Visualizer: simulate and visualize detector-space diffraction patterns to compare expected and observed instrument data.`

## Publications, presentations, awards, and service

Include these selectively. For one-page nonacademic technical resumes, omit awards, full presentation lists, publication sections, and service leadership unless they directly support the role.

For roles where communication matters, use a compact `TEACHING AND TECHNICAL COMMUNICATION` section rather than a full service section.

For one-page teaching-focused resumes, include publications only when a publication is central to the role or requested. Do not let publications crowd out direct teaching evidence.
